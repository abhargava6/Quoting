What's happening: the client names a vehicle by capacity need, not our exact type. We map "SUV or similar, 5–6 pax" to the MPV (cap 6) — exactly what the team did.

Question for the team: is capacity-based mapping the right behavior when a client's vehicle name doesn't match ours?

⚠ Gap — international coverage / missing rates Need your input

What's happening: Bangkok is international; the market/rate may not be on file in Core.

Our approach (proceeding): if a rate is missing, the assistant asks you to supply one, uses it for the quote, and warns it must be added in Core afterward, or the quote will be wrong (or surfaces it for the team to add & re-quote).

Question for the team: which international markets are reliably rated in Core vs. created ad hoc?

⚠ Gap — currency & VAT for international Need your input

What's happening: the client asked for "all taxes/VAT included." We quote in USD today (as the team did), all-in.

Question for the team: should international quotes ever show local currency, and how should VAT be represented vs. our standard all-in/federal-tax model?

✓ Working — EV / driver language as notes; overtime & garage-to-garage surfaced Confirm

What's happening: EV preference and "English-speaking drivers" are captured as notes on the quote. The extra-hour rate = the market hourly rate, and the quoted time already includes garage-to-garage (so 12 "as-directed" hours quotes at ~13.5h, as the team's quote showed).

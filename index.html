What it does

You talk to an assistant in plain English. It reads the request, asks you only what it needs, looks up the actual pricing from Savoya's system (your client's negotiated rates, special-event pricing, transfer rates — whatever applies), and hands you back a quote you can send. If a request can be fulfilled more than one way, it shows options to compare. You can apply a discount right in the chat, and pricing is presented all-in (fuel surcharge + federal tax included; incidentals at cost).

What it solves today

✗Hours per quote. Big multi-day, multi-city requests take forever to price by hand.
✗Rate hunting. Looking up rack, special-event, and transfer rates across markets one by one.
✗Easy-to-miss money. A special-event rate or a missing transfer rate that should've applied.
✗Manual documents. Rebuilding the same quote layout every time.
✗Guesswork on big groups. Figuring out how many vehicles of which type to move 120 people.

How it works — 3 steps

1

Paste the request

The client's email, a few lines of text, or a manifest — however it came in.

2

Answer a few questions

Just what's needed: which client, how many hours, any VIPs, which city, etc.

3

Get a client-ready quote

Review it, apply a discount, then send as a branded PDF, deck, or spreadsheet.

Good to know: this pilot is about quoting — it does not book or change reservations, and it doesn't replace Convey. For manifests you'll still clean them in Convey first, then drop the result here for pricing. All prices in these examples are illustrative unless noted.
